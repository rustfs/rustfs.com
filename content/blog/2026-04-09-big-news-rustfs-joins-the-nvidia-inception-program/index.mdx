We are thrilled to announce that **RustFS** has officially joined the [**NVIDIA Inception program**](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/startups). This marks a pivotal milestone for us, serving as powerful validation of the progress we’ve made over the past nine months and the growing market recognition of our vision.

> **Current status:** Program membership is not a technical certification or product endorsement. RustFS remains in public beta, while RDMA and DPU work described below remains roadmap direction.

RustFS is an S3-compatible object storage system built in **Rust** and released under the **Apache 2.0 license**. Teams considering it as an alternative to MinIO should verify the exact S3 operations, migration steps, and operational behavior their workloads require.

![](/images/blog/big-news-rustfs-joins-the-nvidia-inception-program/rustfs-key-data-summary-1-1024x572.png)

### RustFS in the AI Era

Object storage is rapidly becoming the backbone of the AI era. From large-scale model training and AI application development to the persistent memory of AI agents, object storage is everywhere. We believe this represents a massive opportunity for RustFS.

Our vision for AI-native storage involves offloading the network stack (**RDMA**) and compute-intensive tasks (such as **erasure coding, compression, and encryption**) directly to the **DPU**. By doing so, we ensure that data storage is no longer a bottleneck for AI workloads, significantly driving up overall computational efficiency.

Through the NVIDIA Inception program, the project can explore NVIDIA platforms and technical resources while keeping any resulting AI-oriented features subject to normal open development, testing, and release status.

### What’s Next?

The project has since entered **public beta**. Current maturity and feature availability should be read from the [repository status](https://github.com/rustfs/rustfs#feature--status), not the older schedule published with this announcement.

We currently use GitHub as our primary feedback channel. If you encounter any issues while using RustFS, please let us know by opening an **[issue](https://github.com/rustfs/rustfs/issues)** on our [rustfs/rustfs](https://github.com/rustfs/rustfs). We also warmly welcome contributions via [pull requests](https://github.com/rustfs/rustfs/pulls).

Stay tuned for more updates as we continue to redefine high-performance storage!
